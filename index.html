Mesquite & Brush Mitigation

Open land. Clean lines. Lasting results.

Imagine looking across your property and finally seeing your land again; not dense scrub or dying brush, but open grassland stretching to the horizon. That's what we do.

We work year-round to reveal the beauty beneath the brush, restoring grazeable acres and improving access by removing invasive or nuisance species. We specialize in mesquite mitigation for North Texas pastures and rangeland.

Mesquite resprouts from buds at the root collar even after cutting. We cut each trunk flush to — or slightly below — ground level and immediately treat the stump and collar with a specialized herbicide mix to prevent regrowth.

This focused approach avoids the cost, disruption, and scarring of deep grubbing or broad foliar spraying, giving you cleaner results that last.

A different approach

We tailor the treatment for each property to each species.

We specialize in mesquite mitigation and also treat a range of other invasive or nuisance species, including huisache, yaupon holly, lotebush, Chinese tallow, ligustrum, cedar elm, Macartney rose, pricklypear, and more.

Every property is different and so is every plant. We tailor each treatment using cut-stem, basal bark, or selective foliar applications to ensure effective control with minimal disturbance to desirable vegetation.

Benefits to your land

  1. Instantly improve your view with a cleaner, natural look
  2. No more dense thickets or half-dead brush and without widespread soil disturbance
  3. More usable pasture and better forage access for cattle and horses
  4. Reduced tire and equipment hazards and improved road and fenceline access
  5. Targeted, species-specific treatment protects desirable trees and grasses

What to expect

  1. On-site walk-through to identify target species and set priorities
  2. Crew removes target brush and treats stumps immediately; minimal rutting/soil disturbance
  3. Photo documentation and simple treatment log
  4. Follow-up inspection at 6-12 months; retreatment of any resprouts in scope
  5. Normal grazing typically can continue; we'll advise on any label-specific restrictions
